# Audio Demo Scripts — AI Voice Only

One-sided recordings. AI receptionist "Sarah" only. Pauses where the customer is speaking. Use ElevenLabs with emotion/direction tags.

---

## Demo 1: Daytime Booking Call

Thanks for calling Mike's Heating and Air, this is Sarah. How can I help you today?

[pause]

Oh no, yeah that's no fun — especially with this heat. Let me get you taken care of. Can I grab your name?

[pause]

Got it, Rick. And what's the best number to reach you at?

[pause]

Perfect. And the address where the unit's at?

[pause]

Okay. And just so the tech knows what he's walking into — is this a central air system or a window unit?

[pause]

Got it. Is the unit running at all, or completely off?

[pause]

Okay so the fan's going but just blowing warm. [reassuringly] That helps narrow it down — could be a refrigerant issue, could be the compressor. The tech'll diagnose it on site. I've got an opening tomorrow morning between 8 and 10, or Thursday afternoon between 1 and 3. What works better for you?

[pause]

Done. You're booked for tomorrow, Wednesday, between 8 and 10 AM. Tech'll call you about 30 minutes before he heads your way. [warmly] You're gonna get a confirmation text here in just a second with all the details.

[pause]

Diagnostic fee is 89 bucks, and that gets applied to the repair if you decide to go ahead with it. No surprises.

[pause]

You're welcome, Rick. We'll get you cooled down. Have a good one.

## Demo 2: After-Hours Emergency

Thanks for calling Mike's Heating and Air, this is Sarah. We're currently after hours, but I can absolutely help you. What's going on?

[pause]

Oh gosh. [seriously] Okay — furnace out, 20 degrees outside, two kids in the house. I hear you. Let's get someone out there. What's your name?

[pause]

Lisa, what's your address?

[pause]

And best phone number?

[pause]

Got it. Couple quick questions so I can relay this to the tech. You said it was making a banging noise — did you notice any unusual smells at all? Anything like gas or burning?

[pause]

[relieved] Okay good, that's good. And your thermostat — is it showing anything or is the screen blank?

[pause]

58 and dropping. Alright Lisa, here's what I'm gonna do. I'm sending this over to our on-call technician right now. [confidently] He's going to call you back within 15 minutes to get out to you tonight. You'll also get a text confirming he's been notified.

[pause]

[warmly] That's what we're here for. In the meantime — if you have any space heaters, get those going in one room, keep the kids in there. And Lisa, if you notice a gas smell at any point, [firmly] get everyone out first and call 911. Okay?

[pause]

You're welcome. Help is on the way. Stay warm.

## Recording Notes

**Voice:** Female, mid-30s. Warm but sharp. Like the best office manager you've ever met — the kind who runs the whole shop and everyone knows it.

**Key direction:**
- NOT robotic, NOT overly cheerful customer service voice
- Natural contractions: "gonna," "tech'll," "89 bucks" not "eighty-nine dollars"
- Slight reactions to what the customer "says" — the "oh gosh" and "okay good" make it feel like a real conversation, not a script
- Demo 2 should feel slightly more urgent — she's still calm and in control, but she takes it seriously

**Pauses:** Keep them short — 1.5 to 2.5 seconds. Just enough to feel like someone talked. Don't let it drag.

**Post-production:**
- Light phone compression on the AI voice to match real call quality
- Optional: very faint room tone / phone line hiss underneath
- No music, no intro, no outro — just the call
